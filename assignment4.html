This week's assignment took a deep look at email, sending messages, and programs about email. The first thing I feel needs to be talked about is email scams and phishing. To start, phishing is a way of trying to get someone's personal information by acting as an official company. For example, you get an email that you just won 100$ from amazon but they need you to log in to your Amazon account to get your money, they then take your information and often take money from your account. There are tons of unique ways people try and pull email scams like this and it's a huge problem and responsible for millions stolen every year. I learned that if it's a legit company they will never ask for your information. I see emails like this sometimes but have never fallen for one of these traps.

The next email-related program that I was curious about was Mozilla Thunderbird. I learned that Mozilla Thunderbird is a free open-source email client that was made by the Mozilla foundation. It has features such as a news feed, chat, email encryption, and a personal information manager. Thunderbird can also be used across platforms like Linux, Windows, and macOS. Overall this is a solid email alternative to the likes of Google, AOL, and yahoo. I can see myself setting up a business account using this email service.

The third topic I was curious about was email spoofing. To quickly go over, email spoofing is the creation of an email with a forged sender address. For example, you get an email from your mother saying she needs one thousand dollars and to send it right away. This is not only a scam but an email spoof. I also learned there are countermeasures that can be implemented. Sender Policy Framework (SPF) is a email authenticator designed to detect forged sender addresses. Some of these countermeasures are built into apps like Gmail. The way I envision myself using this information is just being generally aware these scams are out there and knowing what to avoid when opening an email that looks dangerous. Email safety is a big deal in this day and age and a huge part of nearly every job you get out in the world, and it's important to know how to stay safe with emails.
